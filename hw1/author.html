Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm

Jacob Grimm and Wilhelm Grimm are two German brothers that collaborated and produced some of the best-known folktales in the 19th century. They were both philologists, lexicographers and cultural researchers. Their stories, while called "Fairy Tales", are often of the grim nature and might not be suitable for children. They attended University of Marburg where they researched early history of German language and literature. The resurgence of Romanticism in the 18th century revived interest in traditional folk lore. They popularized traditional oral folktales like Cinderella, The Frog Prince, Hansel and Gretel, Rapunzel, Sleeping Beauty, and Snow White.
